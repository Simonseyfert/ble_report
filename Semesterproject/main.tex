\section*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Abstract}

Fully implantable neural interfaces require both wireless power delivery and wireless
data transmission to eliminate transcutaneous connections and the associated infection
risk, patient discomfort, and long-term reliability concerns. This work presents a
complete wireless neural recording and stimulation system targeting electrocorticography
and spinal cord surface recordings, with the rat spinal cord as the primary application.
The system comprises a peripheral nRF52832 module interfacing with an Intan RHS2116
16-channel neural recording and stimulation chip via SPI, a central nRF52832 device
receiving data over Bluetooth Low Energy~(BLE), and a Python-based graphical user
interface for real-time visualisation, configuration, and stimulation control.

The central design contribution is the replacement of the CPU-polling acquisition loop
used in a prior single-channel implementation with a fully hardware-driven pipeline based
on the nRF52832's Programmable Peripheral Interconnect~(PPI) subsystem and EasyDMA.
This decouples data acquisition almost entirely from CPU availability, eliminating
sample jitter caused by SoftDevice radio preemption and enabling the processor to remain
in low-power sleep between BLE connection events. The system supports a configurable
selection of 1--16 recording channels at adjustable per-channel sampling rates up to
32\,kHz, charge-balanced biphasic electrical stimulation with per-channel amplitude and
timing control, and persistent configuration storage in non-volatile flash memory.

BLE throughput with real SPI data reached up to approximately 540\,kbps in the
16-channel, 2\,kHz-per-channel configuration, confirming that the hardware-driven
pipeline sustains the required data rate. Faithful reconstruction of a 300\,Hz,
1\,mV sinusoidal test signal was demonstrated across all 16 channels simultaneously.
The nRF52832 peripheral module consumed 18.6\,mW (equivalent to 20.4\,mW at 3.3\,V),
a 31\,\% reduction compared to the prior single-channel implementation, while
simultaneously increasing the channel count 16-fold. Combined system power
(nRF52832\,+\,Intan RHS2116) was 61.3\,mW at 3.6\,V. End-to-end operation under
wireless power was demonstrated with a compact WLCSP49 nRF52832 module. Electromagnetic
interference measurements identified external carrier board wiring as the dominant
noise pickup mechanism, with interference reduced more than three-fold by shorting
a channel directly on the Intan die, pointing to PCB co-integration as the decisive
next step toward WPT compatibility.
